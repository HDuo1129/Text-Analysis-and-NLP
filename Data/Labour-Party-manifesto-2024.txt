LABOUR PARTY MANIFESTO 2024: CHANGE

I. MY PLAN FOR CHANGE
This election is about change. A chance to stop the endless Conservative chaos that has directly harmed the finances of every family in Britain. A moment where we can turn the page on a set of ideas that, over 14 years, have consistently left us more vulnerable in an increasingly volatile world.

And an opportunity to begin the work of national renewal. A rebuilding of our country, so that it once again serves the interests of working people.

RESTORE HOPE
Every great nation is held together by shared beliefs. 
1. Politics should be driven by a sense of service to the country, not considerations of party or self-interest.
2. If you work hard then - whoever you are, wherever you started in life - Britain is a country that will respect your contribution and give you a fair chance to get on.

STOP THE CHAOS
To restore this hope, we must first end the Conservative chaos. The visible decline in our communities – people waiting on trolleys in A&E; families weighed down by soaring mortgages; sewage in our rivers – is not an accident. Only a Labour government can break this cycle.

--------------------------------------------------
II. MY FIRST STEPS FOR CHANGE
--------------------------------------------------

1. DELIVER ECONOMIC STABILITY: Tough spending rules to grow our economy and keep taxes, inflation, and mortgages as low as possible.
2. CUT NHS WAITING TIMES: 40,000 more appointments each week, during evenings and weekends, paid for by cracking down on tax avoidance.
3. LAUNCH BORDER SECURITY COMMAND: Specialist investigators and counter-terror powers to smash the criminal boat gangs.
4. SET UP GREAT BRITISH ENERGY: A publicly-owned clean power company to cut bills and boost energy security.
5. CRACK DOWN ON ANTISOCIAL BEHAVIOUR: More neighbourhood police and tough new penalties for offenders.
6. RECRUIT 6,500 NEW TEACHERS: Paid for by ending tax breaks for private schools.

--------------------------------------------------
III. MISSION-DRIVEN GOVERNMENT
--------------------------------------------------

Labour's five national missions:
1. KICKSTART ECONOMIC GROWTH: Secure the highest sustained growth in the G7.
2. CLEAN ENERGY SUPERPOWER: Zero-carbon electricity by 2030.
3. TAKE BACK OUR STREETS: Halve serious violent crime and raise confidence in police.
4. BREAK DOWN BARRIERS TO OPPORTUNITY: Reform childcare and education systems.
5. BUILD AN NHS FIT FOR THE FUTURE: Reduce lives lost to the biggest killers.

--------------------------------------------------
IV. STRONG FOUNDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------

NATIONAL SECURITY
- Unshakeable commitment to the UK's nuclear deterrent and NATO.
- Conduct a Strategic Defence Review within the first year.
- Set a path to spending 2.5% of GDP on defence.
- Introduce 'Martyn's Law' to strengthen security at public venues.

SECURE BORDERS
- Create a new Border Security Command.
- End the Rwanda partnership and use the savings to smash smuggling gangs.
- Hire 1,000 additional returns and enforcement staff to fast-track removals.

ECONOMIC STABILITY
- Strict fiscal rules: budget must move into balance; debt must be falling by the fifth year.
- No increase in National Insurance, Income Tax (basic, higher, or additional rates), or VAT.
- Strengthen the Office for Budget Responsibility (OBR).
- Appoint a Covid Corruption Commissioner to recoup lost public money.

--------------------------------------------------
V. KICKSTART ECONOMIC GROWTH
--------------------------------------------------

- INDUSTRIAL STRATEGY: A new partnership with business via a statutory Industrial Strategy Council.
- NATIONAL WEALTH FUND: £7.3 billion to invest in ports (£1.8bn), gigafactories (£1.5bn), and steel (£2.5bn).
- PLANNING REFORM: Build 1.5 million new homes. Restore mandatory targets and prioritize brownfield/grey-belt land.
- INFRASTRUCTURE: A ten-year strategy to end project delays and fix 1 million additional potholes per year.
- RAILWAYS: Bring railways into public ownership as contracts expire. Create 'Great British Railways'.
- WORKERS' RIGHTS: Implement the 'New Deal for Working People' within 100 days. Ban exploitative zero-hours contracts and end 'fire and rehire'.

--------------------------------------------------
VI. CLEAN ENERGY SUPERPOWER
--------------------------------------------------

- GREAT BRITISH ENERGY: Publicly-owned company headquartered in Scotland.
- TARGETS: Double onshore wind, triple solar, and quadruple offshore wind by 2030.
- WARM HOMES PLAN: £6.6 billion investment to upgrade 5 million homes with insulation and solar.
- NORTH SEA: No new licences for new oil and gas fields. Protect existing jobs and manage existing fields.
- WATER: Put failing water companies under special measures. Block bonuses for executives who pollute rivers.

--------------------------------------------------
VII. TAKE BACK OUR STREETS
--------------------------------------------------

- NEIGHBOURHOOD POLICING: 13,000 additional officers and PCSOs.
- RESPECT ORDERS: New powers to ban persistent offenders from town centres.
- KNIFE CRIME: Halve knife crime in a decade. Ban ninja swords and lethal blades.
- VIOLENCE AGAINST WOMEN: Specialist rape units in every force. Domestic abuse experts in 999 control rooms.
- RETAIL CRIME: Create a specific offence for assaults on shopworkers.

--------------------------------------------------
VIII. BREAK DOWN BARRIERS TO OPPORTUNITY
--------------------------------------------------

- EARLY YEARS: 3,000 new primary school-based nurseries.
- SCHOOLS: Free breakfast clubs in every primary school. Limit the number of branded uniform items to cut costs.
- CURRICULUM: Review curriculum to include more creative, digital, and speaking skills.
- SKILLS: Replace the Apprenticeships Levy with a flexible Growth and Skills Levy.
- HIGHER EDUCATION: Secure the future of the sector and improve access to universities.

--------------------------------------------------
IX. BUILD AN NHS FIT FOR THE FUTURE
--------------------------------------------------

- WAITING LISTS: 40,000 more appointments every week. Use spare capacity in the independent sector to clear the backlog.
- TECHNOLOGY: Double the number of CT and MRI scanners. Use AI for faster diagnosis.
- PRIMARY CARE: Return of the family doctor. End the 8am scramble for GP appointments.
- DENTISTRY: 700,000 more urgent dental appointments. 
- SOCIAL CARE: Establish a National Care Service with national standards and a Fair Pay Agreement for workers.

--------------------------------------------------
X. SERVING THE COUNTRY
--------------------------------------------------

- ETHICS: New independent Ethics and Integrity Commission.
- HOUSE OF LORDS: Immediate reform to remove the right of hereditary peers to sit and vote. Mandatory retirement at 80.
- VOTING: Give 16 and 17-year-olds the right to vote in all elections.
- DEVOLUTION: Landmark devolution legislation to transfer power out of Westminster to local communities.
- NORTHERN IRELAND: Repeal and replace the Legacy Act.

--------------------------------------------------
XI. BRITAIN RECONNECTED
--------------------------------------------------

- EUROPE: Reset the relationship with the EU. No return to the single market or customs union, but tear down unnecessary trade barriers.
- UKRAINE: Steadfast military and diplomatic support against Russian aggression.
- MIDDLE EAST: Push for an immediate ceasefire in Gaza and the release of all hostages. Commitment to recognizing a Palestinian state as part of a two-state solution.
- DEVELOPMENT: Restore the 0.7% GNI target for international development when fiscal circumstances allow.
